About

Junkyard bash is a 1-4 player racing game in a sci-fi junkyard.

The players take control over four different characters and race down a wacky, scrappy racetrack, while trying to bash their opponents off the map.


The game is split-screen and played with controllers.


Be the first to reach the finish line!


Level Design

The main goal of the level design was creating levels that progressively got harder, and that could be connected as parts of a bigger race track. We wanted the levels to be challenging and fun.


I achieved this by lots of researching and playtesting on the theme, and finding what I thought was fun with racing games. I then replicated the fast feeling and sliding curves by quick prototyping with the landscape spline tool.


The objective is trying to bash your opponents off the map, and be the first to reach the finish line.


Level X

• Starts easy    • Tight curves    • Protecting edges


1. With this being my first time attempting level design, now in hindsight I see a lot of areas where I could improve on all the levels. The biggest issue is the lack of contrast. I tried using beams and arches to frame in the path, but in my opinion it only works to an extent.


2. I tried to the lead the player by using light sand material in the middle of the race tracks, aswell as shaping the actual tracks in a guiding way. If I would redo it now, I'd pick a material that had a much higher contrast to the surroundings.


3. This level is in the beginning of the game. To make the level more forgiving and easier for the players, as they are not comfortable with the mechancis yet, I sculpted high borders to prevent falling off. The only challenges in this level are the curves, and your opponents.


Level X

• Level splits up    • "Hidden" shortcut route    • Balancing all sides    • Introducing propeller


I wanted the level to split up in different lanes. I made two of them more obvious, and then hid the middle path with debris and foliage. The players can see each other on the other paths while on one, to make it feel more competitive.


1.The middle path is a shortcut, but it's also harder. It features a large rotating propeller and more hazards, which requires a higher skillcap. This path is optional, and I wanted to add it for players who wanted to explore and challenge themselves.


2. The side paths are the same length. To ensure that they were balanced, the one that starts with the path downhill ends with it going uphill, and vice versa.


What I would have done better. If I would redo this now, I'd add elements with leading lines to communicate where the path continues around corners. I'd change the shape of the path to smoothen it out and lead the player better.

Level X

• Multiple different paths    • Harder    • Balancing all sides    • Using "jump" mechanics


1. Again, the level splits up. The longer path compensates for the length in being slightly easier as well as having speed boosters before jumping over an obstacle.


2. This path is a shortcut. It's hidden it in plain view by foliage and the shape of the level. It's harder, as its more narrow, but at the end the player is rewarded with a speedboost.


3. The shortest path (3) is also the harder path out of the 2 main ones. Having learned the ability before, the player now has to jump over the fire pit or drive on a narrow plank before continuing.


4. The players always meet up together at the end of the levels. It was important to always funnel them back together, so that they could use their bash mechanic on each other as well as boosting the competitive feeling.


What I would have done better. I would have made the race track a lot smoother as well as making contrasts. I would also space it out more, and just in general make the tracks wider. The right side is a bit too hard. I would remove the jump and make the bridge slightly larger and longer instead, featuring obstacles the player need to dodge.

Level X

• More linear    • Rotating propellers    • Shortcut    • Challenging curves


1. This level is more linear, and the biggest challenge here is other players and speed. The curves are sharp, and if the player doesn't watch their driving they could drive off or get pushed off. Of course the respawn is far away, but it still could cost them the first place title!


2. Most of my levels have shortcuts or alternative paths. This one is less obvious. Once someone collides with the wooden railings, they enable physics and fall off. In this level, it lets the player that is last jump over and catch up with the rest.


(Unlit mode pictures to see the structural contrasts, the level works in unlit mode too)


3. Here used as a catch up mechanic, the pile of boxes slightly slows down whoever drives into them first. To not feel unfair to the player that is first, it's only very slight.


4. The big propeller is rotating, and the player has to match between the propeller blades to not get pushed away and potentially off.


What I would have done better. If I had remade this level, I would have focused more on contrasts. I like the general level flow, but again the contrasts are too low and it's not communicated well to the player where the track begins and ends. I'd like a metal railing which had horizontal lines instead of the vertical plank barricades, and change the track material to something quite a bit darker, like stone.

Final level

• One final challenge    • Had to feel rewarding


1. I didn't want to make the last level hard. It needed to feel rewarding, and making it too hard would be annoying for the player. Here the player needs to dodge or drive through the obstacles. Again, driving through them would very slightly slow them down.


2. I communicated the sharp turn by placing contrasting fire, which the player knows they should avoid. I tried to make the shape of the path as a guiding element, having the outer edge higher up and the tight corners lower down.


3. Finishing the game had to feel rewarding, as it was the climax of the game. I added lots of speed boosts to make the player feel really fast and powerful. The finish "line" is high up on a hill, so that it could be used as a landmark when progressing closer.
